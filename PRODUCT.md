# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Working Canadian power engineers and plant operators — journeymen, 4th/3rd-class engineers, boiler operators — who know the work and need the paper. They arrive after shifts, from job sites, from plants. They're not exploring the industry; they've already decided. They're evaluating whether FSA is the right prep tool for their exam paper.

## Product Purpose

Full Steam Ahead is a Power Engineering exam prep platform for SOPEEC 2nd Class (six papers: 2A1–2B3) and 3rd Class candidates. The marketing site exists to convert qualified operators into $149/month subscribers. Success looks like a visitor who recognizes that FSA was built by someone who understands their world — not a corporate product, not a textbook — and clicks Enroll.

## Brand Personality

Gritty, direct, built-tough. Industrial character earned, not performed. The aesthetic mirrors the work itself: high-stakes, low-tolerance for fluff, built to withstand. Russ is the brand — a real engineer who's been there. Every word and surface should feel like it came from that credibility, not from a marketing team.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS / startup** (purple gradients, glassmorphism, TailwindUI templates): looks like every B2B tool — kills credibility with operators in seconds.
- **Academic / textbook** (white backgrounds, institutional blues, clip art): exactly what FSA replaces — evokes SOPEEC textbooks and university LMS dread.
- **Over-produced e-learning** (Coursera/Udemy energy — stock photos of smiling students, gamification badges, corporate cheerfulness): operators see through it; it signals "not for us."
- **Trades bootcamp bro** (red/black, aggressive CTAs, gym-supplement energy): too try-hard — confuses gritty with loud. FSA is confident, not screaming.

## Design Principles

1. **Earned, not announced.** Credibility comes from specificity — paper codes, real exam structures, operator context — not from bold claims or big stock imagery. Show the product; let the detail do the work.
2. **Industrial restraint.** The dark carbon palette and orange accent carry the aesthetic. Don't over-design; the job site doesn't have animations for everything.
3. **Russ speaks.** Every heading, CTA, and body copy should sound like a guy who's run a boiler room and passed the 2nd Class — not like a copywriter who read about it. Avoid jargon-in-a-suit (synergize, optimize your learning journey) and watered-down hedging.
4. **Operators are busy.** Information hierarchy matters. They scan; they decide fast. Long explanations lose them. The hierarchy must communicate the core offer (what paper, what prep, what price) before they can scroll.
5. **The stakes are real.** These exams determine career advancement and wages. The interface should hold that weight — confident, precise, no punchlines.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Focus-visible styles on all interactive elements (already implemented via `--orange` outline). Body text contrast ≥4.5:1 against `--carbon` (#0D1117). Reduced motion must suppress all scroll-triggered entrance animations.
