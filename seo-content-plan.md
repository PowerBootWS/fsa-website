# FSA SEO Content & Authority Plan
_Last updated: 2026-06-21_

## Situation

All current traffic is branded (fullsteamahead.ca = 83% CTR on 42 impressions). Non-branded articles have near-zero clicks — 33 pages indexed, none ranking reliably above position 20 except a handful with tiny impression counts. The article library is broad but shallow relative to what Google needs to see topical authority.

| /articles/2nd-class-power-engineering-salary-canada/ | 38.1 | 17 | Salary queries have real volume |

**Root problem:** Google doesn't see FSA as a topical authority on power engineering exam prep yet. The pillar pages exist but there's almost no specific, deep content on individual SOPEEC papers — which is exactly what near-purchase candidates search for.

---

## Strategy

Three parallel tracks, each compounding on the others:

1. **Topical depth** — per-paper SOPEEC content that no competitor has written at this level
2. **Geo/salary pages** — low-difficulty, real search volume, zero current coverage
3. **Community seeding** — /library.html as a genuine resource to earn links and direct traffic from Reddit/forums

---

## Track 1 — Per-Paper SOPEEC Pages

Each 2nd class paper (2A1–2B3) and 3rd class paper cluster gets its own dedicated page. These are the highest-intent queries in the entire niche. Someone searching "SOPEEC 2A1 practice questions" is days away from signing up.

No one publishes exam-paper-specific prep content at this depth — PanGlobal sold textbooks, not strategy guides. This is the clearest topical authority gap to fill.

### Hub page (build first)

**Slug:** `/articles/sopeec-2nd-class-exam-papers/`
**Primary keyword:** "SOPEEC 2nd class papers" / "SOPEEC exam papers"
**Format:** Overview of all six 2nd class papers — what each covers, typical difficulty, recommended order, how FSA's practice questions map to each paper. Links out to all six individual paper pages below.
**Why first:** Hub gives Google a single page to anchor the cluster. Individual paper pages inherit its authority via internal links.

### Individual paper pages (build in this order)

| Priority | Slug | Primary keyword | Why this order |
|----------|------|-----------------|---------------|
| 1 | `/articles/sopeec-2a1-exam-guide/` | "SOPEEC 2A1" / "2A1 power engineering exam" | First paper most candidates sit; highest search intent |
| 2 | `/articles/sopeec-2b1-exam-guide/` | "SOPEEC 2B1" / "2B1 power engineering exam" | B-stream commonly sat alongside A-stream |
| 3 | `/articles/sopeec-2a2-exam-guide/` | "SOPEEC 2A2" / "2A2 power engineering exam" | Sequential from 2A1 |

### What each paper page needs

~1,200–1,500 words covering:
- What the paper covers (topic breakdown: boilers, turbines, refrigeration, etc. as applicable)
- What candidates find hardest and why
- How to sequence study time for this paper
- Common traps in the multiple-choice format specific to this paper
- How FSA's AI tutor and practice questions cover this paper
- Internal links: → hub page, → pillar exam guide, → 1–2 sibling paper pages
- CTA: start the diagnostic or sign up for 2nd class complete

### What geo/salary pages need

~1,000–1,200 words:
- What the job market actually looks like in that province (industries, employers, typical postings)
- Salary ranges (national + provincial) with sources
- How certification level affects salary/hiring in that region
- Honest answer: is it worth pursuing power engineering in [province]?
- Internal links: → /jobs.html (live job board), → /articles/2nd-class-power-engineering-salary-canada/, → relevant exam guide
- CTA: "Ready to get certified? Start with FSA."

## Track 3 — Community Seeding

The /library.html page hosts the free PanGlobal books (CC licence, PanGlobal ceased 2025). This is the single best organic link-building asset FSA has — it's genuinely useful, unique, and free with no paywall. No article pitching needed; just put it in front of the right communities.

### Target communities

| Community | Format | Approach |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| r/PowerEngineering | Post | "Free 2nd class SOPEEC study books — no signup required" — link to /library.html; add context about the CC release |
| r/PowerEngineeringStudents | Post | Same post, adjusted for student audience |
| r/alberta (PE discussion threads) | Comment | Reply to career/certification questions with the library link as a resource |
| PE-specific Discord/Slack servers | Message | If any exist — research first |
| Power engineering trade forums (PowerEngineerForum.com etc.) | Post/thread | Library resource post; also answer exam prep questions linking to relevant articles |
| ABSA / TSSA / TSBC community threads | Comment | Where regulatory discussion happens; add value first |

### Rules for community seeding

- Never lead with FSA or a sales pitch. Lead with the resource.
- Only drop the library link where it's genuinely the best answer to what was asked.
- If someone asks a question that an FSA article answers well, link the article — not the homepage.
- Track where links are placed. If a forum post or comment drives traffic, note it.
- Russ should ideally post or comment in his own voice for r/PowerEngineering — authenticity matters there.

### Reddit monitoring (backlog #4)
Set up r/PowerEngineering keyword alerts (SOPEEC, 2nd class, study resources, exam prep) to surface threads where seeding is appropriate. Build this alongside the community posting, not as a prerequisite.

```
Week 1–2:  SOPEEC hub page + 2A1 paper guide

Week 5–6:  2B2 + 2A3 paper guides; Alberta jobs article
Week 7–8:  2B3 paper guide; 3rd class exam guide; BC + Saskatchewan jobs articles
Week 9–12: Salary Canada article; 4th class guide; NB jobs; ongoing seeding
```

One article per sitting — don't rush. Quality matters more than cadence in this niche.
