## Idea 1: What Squirrels, Beavers, and Geese Can Teach a Power Engineer About Leadership

**Source story:** `fsa-marketing/docs/personal-stories/2026-06-01-gung-ho-leadership.md`
**Status:** Ready to write

**Angle:** Adapt Russ's Gung-Ho story into a third-person article that teaches the three-part leadership framework to shift coordinators and aspiring leaders in industrial settings.

**Key concepts to cover:**
- The way of the squirrel: a team performs when they understand exactly what they're trying to accomplish and why — specific, measurable goals, not mission-statement buzzwords
- The way of the beaver: every contribution matters regardless of seniority or role; the new operator going to do water samples during a crisis is as valuable as the lead operator managing the emergency
- The gift of the geese: constant encouragement during execution + deliberate recognition at the end; "everybody gets a chance to dance with the pretty girl" — make sure your team gets their moment in the spotlight

**Concrete examples to draw from:**
- Control room scene: asking the newest team member for their perspective after the experienced team had already solved the problem
- Steam leak emergency: operator who took on water sampling so the lead operator could focus on the crisis
- Credit-giving practice: publicly redirecting credit to the team member with the original idea, in front of management

**Checklist hook for conclusion:**
1. Does my team know exactly what we're trying to accomplish and why?
2. Does everybody have a part to play, no matter how big or small?
3. Are we encouraging each other as we execute?
4. When we're done — did I stop to recognize what we just did together?

**Target length:** 900–1200 words
**Suggested slug:** `what-squirrels-beavers-geese-teach-power-engineers-about-leadership`
